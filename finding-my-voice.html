Finding My Voice

I was born in Kabirwala, a town where dust carried stories and silence often spoke louder than words. As the eldest, I learned early to hold things together, and perhaps that is where my love for words began, in the unspoken spaces.

Life has always been a conversation between my inner world and the outside. Books and language became my refuge, a place to ask questions without fear and find beauty even in sorrow. Choosing English was never just academic, it was honoring that inner dialogue.

At Air University, I was drawn to stages and gatherings, not for applause, but for connection. Hosting events, leading societies, and creating spaces for voices became my way of living out loud.

Yet beneath it all, I have known anxiety and quiet shadows. They taught me that strength is steady, persistent, and often silent. Today, I carry all these selves, daughter, student, host, dreamer. Every chapter whispers the same truth: language is not just what I study, it is how I survive.
