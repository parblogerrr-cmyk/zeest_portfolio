Flash Fiction: Before Everyone Woke Up

Someone’s alarm rang in the next room, but I was already awake. I hadn't really slept. The hostel was still dark, the kind of dark that feels heavier than silence, and I sat up slowly because my chest ached in that familiar, unexplained way.

I stepped into the corridor. No lights, no voices, just the faint smell of last night’s chai still clinging to the air. I used to like hostel mornings. Lately, they felt like a question I didn’t want to answer.

I walked to the washroom sink, cold metal under my palms, and stared at my own reflection, hair messy, eyes tired, the kind of face that looks like it has something to confess. I almost said it out loud. Almost.

A door creaked behind me. Someone else waking up. The day beginning again.

I straightened up before they saw me, pretending I had just come to wash my face.

“Long night?” she asked casually.

I nodded. It was easier than explaining that some nights just don’t end.

When she left, I lingered a moment longer, letting the tube light flicker above me like it was trying to decide whether to stay on or give up.

Then I returned to my room, quietly, before the hostel fully woke, before I had to act like I had, too.
